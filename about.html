About This Site

This site was built by a UK fitness coach with over 25 years of experience working across multiple sports. That career has included everything from grassroots youth football to elite military preparation, and the beep test has been a constant feature throughout it.

The test comes up everywhere. In school PE lessons where nobody has explained to the students what they are actually measuring. In police recruitment preparation where candidates are training hard but training wrong. In sports pre-seasons where coaches use it to establish a fitness baseline and athletes have no idea how to interpret their score.

Over those 25 years, the same problems kept appearing. People not understanding the rules. People training hard but not training specifically. People failing a fitness test they were fit enough to pass, simply because nobody had given them accurate information.

That is what this site is for.

The calculator uses the Léger 1988 formula — the same one used by sports scientists and fitness professionals worldwide. The score tables are sourced from published normative data. The military and police standards are verified against official sources and updated regularly. The training advice is based on the actual demands of the test, not generic fitness content.

Everything here is free. The tools work on your phone without downloading anything. The content is written for people who need practical, accurate information quickly — not for people who want to read 3000 words before finding out what level they need to reach.

thebeeptest.com has been online since 2006. The current version of the site was built in 2026 with a complete content and technical rebuild, incorporating updated standards, a new interactive calculator and training resources developed from current best practice.

If you have a question not covered on the site, use the contact page.
